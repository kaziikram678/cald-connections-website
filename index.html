About Us

Community-Led. Community-Built.

CALD Connections is a not-for-profit organisation born from the community it serves.

Our Vision

A WA where every person from every background feels at home, connected, and supported.

Our Mission

Free, culturally safe programs that help diverse communities in Perth's northern suburbs connect, grow, and thrive.

Our Story

We Started at a Kitchen Table

CALD Connections was started by community members who had lived the experience of arriving somewhere new. They knew what it felt like to not know where to go, who to trust, or how to ask for help.

We registered as a not-for-profit and built our first programs in Balcatta in 2022. We started small and grew because the community told us what they needed.

Today we serve 500+ community members across Perth's northern suburbs, running free community programs in four languages.

Mental Health & Wellbeing

Mental health looks different in different cultures. Our workshops are designed with that in mind — safe, non-clinical spaces where you can talk, listen, and connect with people who understand your background.

We run group sessions and one-on-one conversations in Arabic, Dari, Swahili, and English. No clinical jargon. No judgment. Seeking support is a sign of strength.

Sessions are led by trained facilitators from Muslim and CALD backgrounds who understand the cultural and religious dimensions of mental wellbeing. We respect the role of faith, family, and community in your healing.

Senior & Women Empowerment

Older community members and women from diverse backgrounds often face isolation, language barriers, and limited social networks. Our programs change that.

Our women's programs are women-only spaces, respecting the needs of Muslim women to have safe, gender-segregated environments.

What is a CALD Program?

Programs Designed With Your Community

CALD stands for Culturally and Linguistically Diverse. In Australia it refers to people from backgrounds other than English-speaking countries — including migrants, refugees, and people who speak another language at home.

CALD programs are community programs designed for people from diverse cultural backgrounds — delivered in community languages, run by people with lived experience.

Our community programs are free. You don't need a referral. You don't need to prove anything. Just come.

Not sure if a program is right for you? Call 0425 136 651 — we'll answer in your language.

FAQs

Frequently Asked Questions

Is CALD Connections a not-for-profit?
Yes. CALD Connections Limited (ABN 74 693 622 187) is a registered Australian not-for-profit. All money goes directly to community programs.
What communities do you support?
We support people from any culturally diverse background — migrants, refugees, people seeking asylum, Muslim families, East African communities, Afghan communities, multicultural seniors, women, and youth in Perth's northern suburbs.
Are programs really free?
Yes. All regular programs are free. Some special events may have a small shared cost for food — we always tell you in advance. No memberships, registration fees, or ongoing costs.
Do I need a referral?
No referral needed. You can contact us directly, walk into a session, or ask a friend to bring you.
Is food at events halal?
Yes. All food and catering at CALD Connections events is halal. We are mindful of the dietary needs of our Muslim community members at all times.
Are there women-only programs?
Yes. Our women's wellness mornings and some senior programs are women-only spaces, respecting the needs of Muslim women for gender-segregated environments. Call us to confirm which sessions are women-only.
How do I join a program?
Call 0425 136 651, email contact@caldconnections.org.au, or just turn up to a session. We recommend calling first so we can welcome you personally.
Can I refer someone?
Yes. Call or email us with the person's name and needs. We'll reach out to them directly in their language.

What Support Is Available for Multicultural Seniors in Perth, WA?

Multicultural seniors in Perth can access free community programs through CALD Connections.
